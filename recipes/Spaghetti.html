Spaghetti



Description

Classic Spaghetti Carbonara is a quintessential Roman pasta dish renowned for its simple yet incredibly rich and creamy sauce. What sets it apart is the absence of cream; its luxurious texture comes from a clever emulsion of eggs (typically yolks, or a mix of whole eggs and yolks), finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and the rendered fat from cured pork.

Ingredients

  • 1 baguette or crusty bread, sliced into 1/2-inch thick pieces
  • 2-3 ripe Roma tomatoes, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (or rubbed on toast)
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 1 tbsp balsamic glaze (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Toast Bread: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Place bread slices on a baking sheet. Drizzle lightly with olive oil and toast in the oven for 5-7 minutes, or until lightly golden and crisp.
  2. Prepare Topping: In a medium bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, chopped basil, minced garlic, 2 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to combine.
  3. Assemble: Spoon the tomato mixture generously onto each toasted bread slice.
  4. Serve: Drizzle with balsamic glaze (if using) and serve immediately.
